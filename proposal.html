Problem Domain

In the realm of modern day parenting, new parents often face a pressing challenge: the availability of high-quality and accessible educational resources for their young children. This issue relates to the broader domain of early childhood education, where vocabulary development plays a pivotal role. Research demonstrates the critical connection between a child's vocabulary growth and overall school achievement [1]. Moreover, a child's vocabulary size in kindergarten serves as a strong predictor of their ability to learn to read [2], highlighting the profound impact of early language skills on educational success.

The more words a child knows, the more information they can access at a young age, fostering critical thinking and a deeper understanding of the world around them. Encouraging children's vocabulary development ensures they acquire the language and literacy skills necessary to thrive in school and beyond. However, despite this issue's importance, an information technology problem needs attention and innovation. Existing websites and apps attempt to address the vocabulary development challenge but often fall short in terms of content quality, accessibility, or user experience.

To bridge this gap effectively, a comprehensive web application is needed to cater to parents and young learners' vocabulary development needs. Spelling Adventures aims to address this problem by providing a valuable tool for parents to facilitate the education of their young children. Through our platform, parents can access a wealth of educational materials and exciting activities to support their child's early learning journey, making nurturing and educating young minds more manageable and enjoyable.

Moreover, for individuals with developmental challenges, early and consistent exposure to vocabulary can offer essential tools to communicate better, comprehend, and engage with their surroundings. Yet, current technological solutions often under-serve these individuals. Our web application aims to address this disparity as well.

App Description

1. Who are the users of the application? That is, who will be accessing the website? What impacts might this app have on minority groups?

Our primary audience would be young children, new English learners, and individuals with developmental disabilities. Our secondary audience would be parents, guardians, and educators who are seeking an effective and engaging tool to supplement traditional learning methods. The app will benefit groups who may lack resources or access to tutoring. The ability to switch word sets will allow for individuals with learning disabilities to have a consistent, accessible way to learn new vocabulary at their own pace.

2. What will the users do with the web app? What kind of interactive features are you expecting to provide? Remember that you app will need to include at least "two and a half" significant features; be sure to explicitly identify and explain what all of them are! Remember: Users will use this web app to learn the English words.

We expect to provide two main and one smalll interactive features:

  • Game: Crossword Puzzles serve as engaging challenges that will help the user think critically about the word and it's definition. If a user is stuck, they can press a help button and a picture cue will assist them. Users can search through pre-defined word categories and select their preferred crossword puzzle categories.
  • Quiz: The quiz format would be filling the letter in the blanks with a provided picture as the cue. Also, we would provide the letters below.
  • Search/Filter: Searching and choosing the wordsets to learn, or the topic of the crossword puzzles.

First, our users or their parents can select the wordset they want to learn by searching in categories. The categories/topics of the wordsets include animals, fruits, and more. After choosing a specific wordset, the user can start their journey of learning new words by flashcards. Our flashcards will be used to introduce and study words. The content of flashcards including audio pronunciations, definitions, usage, and language tips. After learning, users can choose taking the quiz or playing the game for both improving comprehension and entertainment. Successfully passing one quiz or a crossword puzzle game, the user can receiive one star. Earning stars is one way that may motivate our users to take the quiz or play the word game. Accumulating ten stars enables users to progress into next level. To encourage learning a broad vocabulary, a previously completed crossword category will not grant the user another star.

The way that the users are accessing the information in this matter, an authentic API [3] would be able to work well; having access to the words synonyms, antonyms, etc, will be beneficial to the learning aspect. The API referenced is Merriam-Webster's Elementary Dictionary API, which includes vocabulary for grades 3-5, as well as definitions, examples, language tips, pronunciation, and more. Parents, guardians or educators also have the ability to choose between word sets for grades 3-5, 6-8, and 9-11, which can be implemented using Merriam-Webster's Dictionary APIs [4].
